UNRWA was established by the United Nations General Assembly in 1949 following the 1948 Arab-Israeli conflict. It is unique in the UN system as an agency dedicated to a specific refugee population. UNRWA provides education to over 500,000 students, operates 137 primary health care facilities, and delivers humanitarian assistance including food, cash, and shelter.

The agency's existence serves as both a humanitarian lifeline and a political marker of the unresolved [Palestinian](https://humanitariancommons.org/topics/palestine/) refugee question. Unlike UNHCR, which works toward permanent solutions for refugees, UNRWA's mandate is to provide services pending a political resolution of the refugee issue, a resolution that remains elusive after more than seven decades.

The agency is led by Commissioner-General [Philippe Lazzarini](https://humanitariancommons.org/people/philippe-lazzarini/), who has navigated it through one of its most challenging periods. [Pierre Krähenbühl](https://humanitariancommons.org/people/pierre-krahenbuhl/), the current Director-General of the [ICRC](https://humanitariancommons.org/organizations/icrc/), served as UNRWA's Commissioner-General from 2014 to 2019. [Francesca Albanese](https://humanitariancommons.org/people/francesca-albanese/), the UN Special Rapporteur on the occupied Palestinian territories, has been a prominent voice on the rights of Palestinian refugees served by the agency.
